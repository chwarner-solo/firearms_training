# Vision — The Bigger Picture

> This document is a parking lot for where this could go. None of this is being built right now. It exists so good ideas don't get lost and don't derail the current build target.

---

## The Core Insight

Nobody owns the *training relationship* between firearms instructor and student. The space has:

- YouTube / Instagram — content, not structured training
- Shot timer apps — capture splits, nothing else
- USPSA / SCSA scoring — competition only, not skill development
- Notebooks and spreadsheets — most serious shooters today

**The gap**: structured, runnable drill content — tied to a coach/student relationship, with logged performance over time.

---

## The Creator Economy Angle

Think Patreon meets TrainingPeaks, but for firearms training.

- Elite shooters (Jerry Miculek, Lena Miculek, etc.) publish drill libraries
- Instructors publish their curriculum — students subscribe or purchase
- Drills are *runnable* — not just video, not just description — structured steps you run on your iPad at the range
- Performance is logged — instructor can see who's doing the work
- Monetization built in — tips, subscriptions, one-time purchases, free tiers

**The moat**: the structured drill format. Anyone can post a YouTube video. Nobody else has Jerry's actual training regimen in a format you can *run at the range and log your reps against*.

---

## The Marksmanship Collective Connection

This platform is a natural extension of the Collective's philosophy:
- Marksmanship as craft — nothing tribal, nothing political
- Open to anyone who can legally own a firearm
- Discipline-agnostic: pistol, rifle, dry fire, precision

The Collective becomes the *identity* and *community layer*. The app becomes the *training layer*.

Ken's curriculum lives here. His students get access. He can see their progression.

## Instructor-Student Features (future)

- Instructor creates drill library, assigns to students
- Student sees instructor's drills on their device
- Instructor sees student's session logs and progression
- Drill variations — instructor forks a standard drill and customizes for a student
- Feedback loop — student notes on a session, instructor can respond

---

## Social Layer (future)

- Follow instructors and elite shooters
- See what they're working on (training logs, if public)
- Community drill library — submit and share drills
- Endorsement system — Ken endorses a drill, that carries weight

## Platform Considerations (future)

- Flutter mobile app (iOS + Android) — coach and student on same platform
- Cloud sync — sessions sync across devices
- Range mode vs desk mode — different UI contexts
- Offline-first — ranges don't always have WiFi

## The Precision Rifle Extension

The drill model works identically for rifle:
- Progressive distance drills (same model as the 1" circle drill)
- Positional work (prone, supported, unsupported)
- DOPE logging — not just "I did it" but environmental conditions

Curriculum ladder already mapped:
`.243 Win → 6.5 Creedmoor → 7mm PRC → .338 Lapua`
